Crypto-assets and Climate Change Report

2023 | Role: Researcher

Background: Led a joint research project between the Central Bank of Hungary and CFTE, in collaboration with the Crypto Carbon Ratings Institute (CCRI), producing an industry report on crypto assets and sustainability. The report assesses the environmental impact of cryptoassets, outlines data collection and measurement methodologies, highlights real-world sustainability initiatives, and explores potential regulatory frameworks. Managed a team of three interns and coordinated with a central bank director and CCRI specialist to deliver the publication.

Summary: With Bitcoin consuming energy equivalent to countries like the UAE in 2021, the report examines the environmental impact of crypto assets and mitigation strategies. Proof-of-Work (PoW) cryptocurrencies, comprising 80% of the market, generate 95% of crypto-related emissions. Case studies include Ethereum's 2022 "Merge" (99.95% energy reduction via Proof-of-Stake), Celo's carbon-negative blockchain, and Cardano's supply chain tracking. The report advocates for unified data standards, principle-based regulation, energy labeling, and industry-wide cooperation to align crypto growth with climate goals.

Rearchitecting the Global Financial System

2024 | Role: Researcher

Background: Contributed to a thought leadership whitepaper in partnership with the Japan Fintech Festival on key developments in global finance.

Summary: This report draws insights from the 'Rearchitecting the Global Financial System' roundtable at the Japan FinTech Festival 2025, exploring the global financial system's modernization and technological needs. Key themes include:

(1) Global challenges—1.5 billion unbanked individuals, inefficient systems, and high remittance fees requiring strategic overhaul;

(2) Modernization through innovations like India's Aadhaar and Kenya's M-PESA, leveraging blockchain and forward-thinking regulatory policies;

(3) Digital infrastructure—cyber connectivity, digital identity, interoperable payments, and data exchange for economic competitiveness;

(4) Success stories like India's UPI system, emphasizing public-private collaboration, regulatory reform, and consumer engagement to accelerate digital infrastructure adoption.

Building a Thriving Financial and FinTech Ecosystem in Japan

2024 | Role: Researcher

Background: Partnered with Japan Fintech Festival as a whitepaper. Contributed as a researcher and led a team of 2 interns and 1 analyst.

Summary: This report draws insights from the "Unlocking Japan's potential - building a thriving financial and FinTech ecosystem" roundtable at the Japan FinTech Festival, exploring a blueprint for revitalising Japan's financial sector. Key themes include:

(1) Tokyo's journey in the global financial arena—addressing its decline in rankings through FinTech innovation and government support;

(2) Drivers of modern finance hubs—substantial capital, skilled talent, progressive regulations, entrepreneurship mindset, and global interconnectedness;

(3) Japan's strengths and challenges—leveraging government bonds, near-zero interest rates, and regulatory trustworthiness while addressing slow decision-making, underselling culture, and language barriers;

(4) Strategic recommendations—making Tokyo a starting point, integrating Japan's unique culture and technology dedication, and embracing an inclusive approach to attract global talent and investments.
